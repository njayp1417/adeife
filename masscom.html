Comprehensive Study Notes

Journalism Notes

News Writing Structure

The Inverted Pyramid:

  • Lead: Most important information (Who, What, When, Where, Why, How)
  • Body: Supporting details in order of importance
  • Tail: Least important information

Key Elements of a Good Lead:

  • Answers the 5 W's and H
  • 25-35 words maximum
  • Active voice preferred
  • Present tense for recent events
Interview Techniques

Preparation:

  • Research your subject thoroughly
  • Prepare open-ended questions
  • Set up recording equipment
  • Choose appropriate location

During the Interview:

  • Listen actively
  • Ask follow-up questions
  • Take notes even when recording
  • Maintain eye contact

Broadcasting Notes

Radio Production Basics

Elements of Radio:

  • Voice: Clear articulation, proper pace
  • Music: Background, transitions, themes
  • Sound Effects: Atmosphere, emphasis
  • Silence: Dramatic effect, emphasis

Script Writing Tips:

  • Write for the ear, not the eye
  • Use conversational language
  • Include pronunciation guides
  • Time your scripts accurately

Advertising Notes

AIDA Model

A - Attention: Grab the audience's attention

I - Interest: Generate interest in the product/service

D - Desire: Create desire for the product/service

A - Action: Motivate the audience to take action

Creative Strategy Elements:

  • Target audience identification
  • Unique selling proposition (USP)
  • Brand positioning
  • Message strategy

Public Relations Notes

PR Campaign Planning

RACE Model:

  • Research: Situation analysis, audience research
  • Action: Strategy development, planning
  • Communication: Message delivery, media relations
  • Evaluation: Measuring results, feedback

Key PR Tools:

  • Press releases
  • Media kits
  • Press conferences
  • Special events
